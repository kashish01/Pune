Iskcon Temple



ISKCON New Vedic Cultural Center (NVCC) is a temple of Radha-Krishna located in Kondhwa about 12 kilometres (7.5 mi) south of Pune. It was recently inaugurated on the auspicious occasion of Nityananda Tryodshi which falls on 23 February 2013 and the President of India Pranab Mukherjee inaugurated the Vedic Cultural Centre of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) on February 24, at Pune.

The Temple is up of marble.Each pillar and walls of Temple are worth observing.Each pillar has two Gopis and one elephant engraved at their base.Consistency and accuracy are the key points in the Temple's architecture.The gold embossed alter doors give a stunning look to the Temple.The transparent marble flooring which glows in diffused lights make the Temple interior look mavelous. The dome of the Temple is lotus ;it has a beautiful carving of Goddess Radha and Lord Krishna.The three-dimensional wall paintings on the walls of the Temple are a must watch sight.

A visit at iskcon at the midst of the marbles will make you feel at the peace.Ten-minute meditation session in the main hall is conducted to relax mind.The ground floors of the Temple have book shops and some stalls to buy 'Prasad' and sweets.
